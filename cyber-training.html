Cyber exercise for management


A cyber attack is a business event, and not merely a technological one. There is a significant need to manage an attack as its consequences can be devastating to the company, to the point of closure.


One of the main outcomes of a cyber attack on an organization is uncertainty, which leads to chaos and therefore decisions made under pressure that may lead to undesirable results, losses to the company, loss of trust of customers and stakeholders, and exposure of senior executives to personal lawsuits. The variety of doctrines and facades with which an organization's management is required to deal in a complex and often technological event is not the primary aspect of such an event, but rather it’s the business, legal, or media aspect. A systematic, calculated process - in constant consultation with expert lawyers, proper media handling, customers, and the regulatory aspect - is necessary in order to survive such an event. This prevents loss of customer trust and exposing the company and its senior executives to lawsuits.


Goals: Training company management to prepare for the cyber event and its implications on the basis of several parameters:


  • Familiarity with relevant reference scenarios for the company.

  • Dealing with response to the event in real time.

  • Making decisions during a state of uncertainty.

  • Dealing with the event from all perspectives (communications, clients, regulation, insurance, law enforcement, etc.)

  • Recovery and return to routine.


This process will help employees identify gaps in security and draw conclusions from the effects of a cyber event.
