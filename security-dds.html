Drone Detection System







Guidelines for Building The Solution


Ease of use


Detection quality (Low FAR, High threat discovery rate)


Operate the operator - Send an alert to the relevant authorities for immediate handling, instruct operator on pre-defined required actions)

System Work Methodology


In order to ensure system classification, we use triple-factor detection:


Factor 1 – RF behaviour


Factor 2 – Video analytics


Factor 3 – Operator final detection



System Armed (Scanning)


While the system is scanning and there are no drones on scope, the indicator screen stays green

Stage 1 - RF (Radio Frequency) Detection


When a drone is detected by the RF system, the indication screen turns orange


At this stage, the system is now armed and video analytics goes into action for verification



Stage 2 - Analytics Detection


Once the video analytics system detects suspicious movement in the area, it will alert the operator and the section indicator will turn red, marking a verified drone detection

Stage 3 - Operator Final Visualization & Classification


The operator takes control of the PTZ (Pan, Tilt, Zoom) camera and follows the suspected object until they can make a verified and certain identification



Factor 1 - Radio Frequency Signature


Each device has its own communication characteristics: The component we developed studies the RF activity in the air, and classifies and categorizes monitored RF patterns for wireless behaviour, RF signal type, and radio signature


Our system looks for suspected drone patterns, and raises an alert once a drone is within the range of approximately one kilometer

System Benefits


Thanks to the three-factor alert authentication, issued alerts are highly accurate and reliable


The interface is easy to use and intuitive to the user, even for novice operators


Ability to track objects in real-time


Adaptable to the environment
